## 📘 Course Description

This course introduces foundational academic writing skills through two core genres: **description** and **narration**. Students learn how to write vivid, concrete, and organized descriptive essays, as well as coherent and meaningful narratives. Emphasis is placed on **clarity, precision, sensory detail, organization, and revision**.

Students move from skill-building exercises to full essays, and from guided drafting to timed writing performance. The course strengthens language control while fostering expressive confidence and reflective thinking.

### Core Writing Skills Practiced

- Using concrete language instead of vague abstraction  
- Showing through sensory detail, action, and dialogue  
- Organizing paragraphs logically (spatial order, chronological order)  
- Developing characters and atmosphere  
- Writing coherent narratives with structure and emotional impact  
- Revising for clarity, precision, and style  

---

### 思政融入

- 通过人物与事件书写，培养真实表达与责任意识，强调尊重他人与社会观察能力  
- 鼓励通过叙事反思个人成长、家庭情感与社会经验，增强价值认同与文化自信  
- 强调学术诚信与原创表达，反对抄袭与机械模仿  
- 在人物与场景描写中关注中国社会现实与文化情境，提升跨文化表达能力  

---

**Outcome:**  
By the end of the course, you will produce clear, vivid, and well-organized descriptive and narrative essays that demonstrate language precision, structural coherence, and meaningful reflection.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of the course, students can:

1. Write well-developed descriptive essays using sensory detail and precise language.  
2. Apply “show, don’t tell” techniques effectively.  
3. Organize descriptive writing using spatial order and logical structure.  
4. Develop characters through action, behavior, and dialogue.  
5. Write coherent narratives with clear plot structure (beginning–middle–end).  
6. Control pacing through scene and summary.  
7. Use dialogue correctly and naturally.  
8. Revise writing for clarity, organization, and stylistic improvement.  
9. Produce timed writing (descriptive or narrative) under exam conditions.  
10. Produce clear English essays (400–700 words per major assignment).

## 📊 Assessment & Grading Policy

| Component | Weight | Description |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| Attendance & Participation | 10% | Active engagement in discussion, workshops, peer review |
| Descriptive Essay | 10% |  organized, vivid, coherent |
| Narrative Essay | 10% |  structured, meaningful, developed |

| Final Exam | 50% | Timed independent writing |

### Grading Criteria

Major writing assignments are evaluated based on:

- **Content & Development** (clarity, depth, vividness)  
- **Organization** (structure, coherence, transitions)  
- **Language Precision** (word choice, sentence variety)  
- **Grammar & Mechanics**  
- **Revision Quality**

## 📚 Course Requirements & Policies

### Attendance

- Regular attendance is expected.
- More than three unexcused absences may affect the final grade.

### Participation

Students are expected to:
- Contribute actively in workshops and discussions.
- Provide constructive peer feedback.
- Complete all in-class writing tasks.

### Academic Integrity

- All submitted work must be original.
- Plagiarism will result in a failing grade for the assignment.
- AI tools may be used for brainstorming or language support, but:
  - They may **not replace original writing**.
  - Students must revise and take responsibility for final submissions.

### Submission Policy

- Essays must be submitted on time.
- Late submissions may receive grade deductions unless prior approval is granted.

## 🤖 Special Focus: Writing in the Age of AI (for Non-Native Speakers)
**Opportunities:** Brainstorming, language refinement, clarity/grammar feedback, generating counter-arguments to stress-test your stance.  
**Risks:** Plagiarism, overreliance, homogenized voice, shallow reasoning/unsupported claims.  
**Ethics & Integrity:** Disclose AI use; remain the **primary author**; verify facts; cite sources; never submit AI-only work.  

**Best Practices**
- Use AI for **support** (idea prompts, outline checks, language polishing).  
- Keep your **voice**: rewrite outputs in your own words, with your reasoning and examples.  
- **Fact-check** and **source** properly; attach an **AI Use Statement** to assignments when applicable.

## 📝 Portfolio & Major Assignments

### 1️⃣ Descriptive Essay

**Length:** 400–600 words  
**Focus:**  
- Sensory detail  
- Concrete language  
- Logical organization  
- Dominant impression  

Students may choose:
- A person  
- A place  
- A meaningful object  

---

### 2️⃣ Narrative Essay

**Length:** 500–700 words  
**Focus:**  
- Clear plot structure  
- Character development  
- Scene vs summary balance  
- Meaningful reflection  

Students may write:
- A personal experience  
- A fictional short story  



---

### 4️⃣ Final Exam

Timed writing (90 minutes).  

- Write one narrative essay  

The final exam assesses independent writing ability without drafting assistance.
